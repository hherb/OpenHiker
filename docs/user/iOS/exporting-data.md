# Exporting Data

OpenHiker lets you export your hikes in multiple formats for sharing, archiving, or importing into other apps.

## Export Formats

### Quick Summary (Markdown)

A plain-text summary containing:
- Hike name and date
- All statistics (distance, elevation, duration, etc.)
- Waypoints with coordinates and notes
- Your comments

Saved as a `.md` file that you can view in any text editor or notes app.

### Detailed Report (PDF)

A professional multi-page report containing:
- Map with your track overlay
- Elevation profile chart
- Complete statistics table
- Waypoints list
- Photos (if attached)
- Your comments

### GPS Track (GPX)

Standard GPX format containing:
- Track points with latitude, longitude, elevation, and timestamps
- Metadata (hike name, date)

GPX files can be imported into virtually any GPS or mapping application (Garmin Connect, Strava, AllTrails, Google Earth, etc.).

## How to Export

1. Go to the **Hikes** tab
2. Tap a hike to open its detail view
3. Tap the **toolbar menu** (three dots)
4. Select **Export Hike**
5. Choose your format

## Tips

- **Markdown** is great for quick sharing via messaging apps
- **PDF** is ideal for printing or archiving with photos
- **GPX** is best for importing into other hiking/fitness apps
- All exports use your preferred unit system (metric or imperial)
