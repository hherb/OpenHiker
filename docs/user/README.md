# OpenHiker User Manual

Welcome to OpenHiker — a free, open-source offline hiking navigation app for iOS, Apple Watch, macOS, and Android. OpenHiker uses free OpenTopoMap tiles so you can navigate trails without an internet connection or subscription fees.

## What is OpenHiker?

OpenHiker lets you:

- **Download topographic maps** for offline use — no cell signal needed on the trail
- **Plan hiking and cycling routes** with turn-by-turn directions
- **Record your hikes** with GPS tracking, elevation profiles, and health data
- **Navigate with your Apple Watch** — leave your phone in your pack
- **Share routes** with the community or transfer maps between devices peer-to-peer
- **Mark waypoints** for water sources, viewpoints, campsites, and more

## Getting Started (Quick Overview)

### Typical Workflow (Apple Ecosystem)

1. **On your iPhone or Mac:** Download a map region covering your hiking area
2. **Plan a route** using the built-in routing engine (or import a GPX file)
3. **Transfer the map and route to your Apple Watch**
4. **On the trail:** Use your watch for turn-by-turn navigation with offline maps
5. **After your hike:** Review stats, elevation profile, and export a report

### Typical Workflow (Android)

1. **Download a map region** covering your hiking area
2. **Plan a route** using the built-in routing engine (or browse community routes)
3. **Start navigation** with turn-by-turn directions
4. **On the trail:** Record your hike with GPS tracking
5. **After your hike:** Review stats, export GPX or PDF reports

## Map Tile Sources

OpenHiker supports three free tile sources:

| Source | Best For | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **OpenTopoMap** | Hiking | Topographic contour lines, trail markings, shelters — the default for hikers |
| **CyclOSM** | Cycling | Cycling infrastructure, bike routes, surface quality |
| **OpenStreetMap** | General | Standard road map with POIs |

All tiles are sourced from OpenStreetMap contributors and are free to use.

## Data & Privacy

- **No account required** — OpenHiker works completely offline after downloading maps
- **No tracking** — your location data stays on your devices
- **iCloud sync** (Apple platforms) keeps your routes, hikes, and waypoints in sync across iPhone, Watch, and Mac
- **Cloud folder sync** (Android) lets you sync via Google Drive, Dropbox, or any storage provider
- **Open source** — [view the code on GitHub](https://github.com/hherb/OpenHiker)
