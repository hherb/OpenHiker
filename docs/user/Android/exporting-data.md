# Exporting Data

Export your hikes and routes in standard formats for sharing or importing into other apps.

## Export Formats

### GPX (GPS Track)

Standard GPX 1.1 XML format containing:
- Track points with latitude, longitude, elevation, and timestamps
- Metadata: name, description, creator
- Compatible with virtually any GPS or mapping app (Garmin Connect, Strava, Komoot, Google Earth, etc.)

### PDF (Detailed Report)

A two-page PDF report (US Letter size):

**Page 1:**
- Title and date
- Comprehensive statistics table:
  - Distance, elevation gain/loss
  - Duration, walking/resting time
  - Average/max speed
  - Estimated calories

**Page 2:**
- Elevation profile chart with:
  - Filled area under curve
  - Grid lines for both axes
  - Min/max elevation annotations
  - Distance and elevation axis labels

## How to Export

### From a Hike
1. Go to the **Hikes** tab
2. Tap a hike to open its detail view
3. Tap the **Export** button
4. Choose **GPX** or **PDF**
5. The **Android share sheet** opens — save to files, send via email, share to other apps

### From a Route
1. Go to the **Routes** tab
2. Tap a route to open its detail view
3. Tap the **Export** button
4. Choose **GPX** or **PDF**
5. Share via the system share sheet

## Tips

- **GPX** is best for importing into other hiking/fitness apps
- **PDF** is ideal for printing, archiving, or sharing with non-hikers
- All exports use your preferred unit system (metric or imperial)
- Export files are generated locally — no internet required
